His Record

A presidency defined by integrity, resilience, and critical decision-making.

Early Life

Born in Omaha, Nebraska on July 14, 1913, Ford's early years were marked by determination and hard work. He excelled in both academics and athletics at the University of Michigan.

Military Service

Served with distinction in the U.S. Navy during World War II aboard the USS Monterey. Rose to the rank of Lieutenant Commander and earned several commendations.

Congressional Career

Represented Michigan's 5th district for 25 years (1949-1973). Served as House Minority Leader and earned respect from both parties for his integrity.

Vice Presidency

Appointed Vice President in 1973 under the 25th Amendment after Spiro Agnew's resignation. Served with dignity during a turbulent time in American politics.

Presidency

Became the 38th President in 1974. Key achievements include the Helsinki Accords, tackling inflation, and healing the nation after Watergate.

Legacy

Remembered for his integrity, bipartisanship, and crucial role in helping the nation recover from the Watergate crisis. Awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 1999.
